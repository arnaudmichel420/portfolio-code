Massage Sportif Annecy
  • Field :
  • Web Design, Web Development
  • Brief :
  • The client has an aging showcase website to promote his sports massage service and wants to improve his online presence with a more ergonomic, aesthetically pleasing and SEO-optimised website. He wanted to develop his clientele so that he could go full-time. He also felt that his practice was little known in France, so he wanted to promote and explain it better.

Objectives :

  • Website Redesign
  • Increased ranking on Google
  • Online appointment booking to free him from this task
  • Online gift card purchase to automate the process

Tools :

For the web design part, I used Figma to quickly sketch and try different ideas. For the website, I mainly used WordPress and when it wasn't possible to replicate the design or a functionality, I used a combination of CSS and JavaScript with ACF to keep the content easily changeable by the client.

Process :

For the design, I propose different inspirations to the client to see what he likes and if it translates to the project goal. From there I did a first page and sent him to get his feedback, changed a few things and went on to do the rest of the model.

Once the design was validated, I started to replicate the model on WordPress using the best practices for SEO (meta title, h1, li). Like I said above when I was limited by CMS or no plugins suited my needs. I used custom code to develop the functionality I needed like on the booking page it was necessary to control the time range of each massage so that the customer doesn't book the wrong duration.

When the development was finished and the SEO was done, I used Filezilla and phpMyAdmin to migrate the website to the client's server. After that I went to Google Search Console to ask for a crawl of the new version of his website to get his new ranking.
