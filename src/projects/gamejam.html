Gamejam | Godot

  • Field :
  • Game Development
  • Brief :
  • Every year, my school hosts a Game Jam for second-year bachelor's students across all 18 campuses. In teams of six, students have 4.5 days to create a video game from scratch, following a given theme. The competition is divided into three fields: Marketing, Design and Development. Each team must deliver a pitch deck, a trailer and a playable game. This year the theme was 'Adaptability.'

Objectives :

  • Website Redesign
  • Increased ranking on Google
  • Online appointment booking to free him from this task
  • Online gift card purchase to automate the process

Tools :

The game was developed on the Godot game engine with no prior experience with the help of YouTube and ChatGPT with a special prompt I was able to develop/learn fast. The visual assets were made by the rest of the team.

Process :

On the first day, we focused on defining our game concept, designing its gameplay, and researching whether the idea was feasible within the given time frame.

On the second day, I began developing the core mechanics, including character movement, animation transitions, and object pickup. I even had time to add particle effects for healing and damage.

The third day was dedicated to enemy pathfinding, implementing attacks for both the player and enemies, and designing the user interface, including the in-game menu and an introduction cinematic.

On the fourth day, I integrated Level 1, along with sprite animations and audio created by our team, seamlessly combining them with the core mechanics I had developed.

On the final day, I integrated Level 2 and focused on fixing the last bugs before delivering the game at midday.
